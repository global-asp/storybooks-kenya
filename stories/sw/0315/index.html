{% include picturecol.html index="02" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Sakima aliishi na wazazi wake na dada yake wa miaka minne. Waliishi katika shamba la mtu tajiri. Nyumba yao ya nyasi ilikuwa mwisho wa safu ya miti iliyopendeza. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Sakima lived with his parents and his four year old sister. They lived on a rich man's land. Their grass-thatched hut was at the end of a row of trees.

Sakima aliishi na wazazi wake na dada yake wa miaka minne. Waliishi katika shamba la mtu tajiri. Nyumba yao ya nyasi ilikuwa mwisho wa safu ya miti iliyopendeza.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="02" prev="01" next="03" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="03" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Sakima alipokuwa na umri wa miaka mitatu, aliugua na kupoteza uwezo wa kuona. Hata hivyo, Sakima alikuwa mvulana mwenye talanta. {% include textcol_end.html %}

When Sakima was three years old, he fell sick and lost his sight. Sakima was a talented boy.

Sakima alipokuwa na umri wa miaka mitatu, aliugua na kupoteza uwezo wa kuona. Hata hivyo, Sakima alikuwa mvulana mwenye talanta.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="03" prev="02" next="04" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="04" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Sakima alatenda mambo mengi ambayo wavulana wengine wa umri wake hawakufanya. Kwa mfano, angeketi na watu wazima na kujadili mambo muhimu. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Sakima did many things that other six year old boys did not do. For example, he could sit with older members of the village and discuss important matters.

Sakima alatenda mambo mengi ambayo wavulana wengine wa umri wake hawakufanya. Kwa mfano, angeketi na watu wazima na kujadili mambo muhimu.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="04" prev="03" next="05" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="05" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Wazazi wa Sakima walifanya kazi katika nyumba ya yule tajiri. Waliondoka nyumbani asubuhi na mapema na kurudi jioni. Sakima aliachwa na dadake. {% include textcol_end.html %}

The parents of Sakima worked at the rich man's house. They left home early in the morning and returned late in the evening. Sakima was left with his little sister.

Wazazi wa Sakima walifanya kazi katika nyumba ya yule tajiri. Waliondoka nyumbani asubuhi na mapema na kurudi jioni. Sakima aliachwa na dadake.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="05" prev="04" next="06" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="06" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Sakima alipenda kuimba sana nyimbo. Siku moja mamake alimuuliza, “Sakima, unajifunza nyimbo hizi kutoka wapi?” {% include textcol_end.html %}

Sakima loved to sing songs. One day his mother asked him, “Where do you learn these songs from, Sakima?”

Sakima alipenda kuimba sana nyimbo. Siku moja mamake alimuuliza, “Sakima, unajifunza nyimbo hizi kutoka wapi?”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="06" prev="05" next="07" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="07" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Sakima alimjibu, “Nazisikia akilini mwangu kisha naziimba.” Mamake hakumwelewa. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Sakima answered, “They just come, mother. I hear them in my head and then I sing.”

Sakima alimjibu, “Nazisikia akilini mwangu kisha naziimba.” Mamake hakumwelewa.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="07" prev="06" next="08" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="08" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Sakima alipenda kumwimbia dadake hasa alipohisi njaa. Dadake alimsikiliza na kucheza. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Sakima liked to sing for his little sister, especially, if she felt hungry. His sister would listen to him singing his favourite song. She would sway to the soothing tune.

Sakima alipenda kumwimbia dadake hasa alipohisi njaa. Dadake alimsikiliza na kucheza.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="08" prev="07" next="09" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="09" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} “Tafadhali Sakima, imba tena,” dada yake angemsihi. Sakima angekubali na kuimba mara nyingine. {% include textcol_end.html %}

“Can you sing it again and again, Sakima,” his sister would beg him. Sakima would accept and sing it over and over again.

“Tafadhali Sakima, imba tena,” dada yake angemsihi. Sakima angekubali na kuimba mara nyingine.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="09" prev="08" next="10" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="10" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Jioni moja, wazazi wake waliporudi nyumbani, walikuwa kimya sana. Sakima alijua kwamba lazima kulikuwa na jambo mbaya. {% include textcol_end.html %}

One evening when his parents returned home, they were very quiet. Sakima knew that there was something wrong.

Jioni moja, wazazi wake waliporudi nyumbani, walikuwa kimya sana. Sakima alijua kwamba lazima kulikuwa na jambo mbaya.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="10" prev="09" next="11" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="11" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} “Kuna shida gani, mama, baba?” Sakima aliuliza. Sakima aligundua kwamba mwana wa tajiri wao alikuwa amepotea. Tajiri alihuzunika na kuwa na upweke mkubwa. {% include textcol_end.html %}

“What is wrong, mother, father?” Sakima asked. Sakima learned that the rich man's son was missing. The man was very sad and lonely.

“Kuna shida gani, mama, baba?” Sakima aliuliza. Sakima aligundua kwamba mwana wa tajiri wao alikuwa amepotea. Tajiri alihuzunika na kuwa na upweke mkubwa.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="11" prev="10" next="12" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="12" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} “Labda atafurahi tena nikimwimbia,” Sakima aliwaambia wazazi wake. Wazazi wake walidharau wazo lake. “Yeye ni tajiri. Wewe ni mvulana asiyeona. Unadhani wimbo wako utamsaidia?” {% include textcol_end.html %}

“I can sing for him. He might be happy again,” Sakima told his parents. But his parents dismissed him. “He is very rich. You are only a blind boy. Do you think your song will help him?”

“Labda atafurahi tena nikimwimbia,” Sakima aliwaambia wazazi wake. Wazazi wake walidharau wazo lake. “Yeye ni tajiri. Wewe ni mvulana asiyeona. Unadhani wimbo wako utamsaidia?”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="12" prev="11" next="13" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="13" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Hata hivyo, Sakima hakukata tamaa. Dada yake vile vile alimpa moyo, “Nyimbo za Sakima hunituliza mimi nikiwa na njaa. Zitamtuliza tajiri vile vile.” {% include textcol_end.html %}

However, Sakima did not give up. His little sister supported him. She said, “Sakima's songs soothe me when I am hungry. They will soothe the rich man too.”

Hata hivyo, Sakima hakukata tamaa. Dada yake vile vile alimpa moyo, “Nyimbo za Sakima hunituliza mimi nikiwa na njaa. Zitamtuliza tajiri vile vile.”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="13" prev="12" next="14" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="14" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Siku iliyofuata, Sakima alimuuliza dadake amwongoze hadi kwenye nyumba ya tajiri. {% include textcol_end.html %}

The following day, Sakima asked his little sister to lead him to the rich man's house.

Siku iliyofuata, Sakima alimuuliza dadake amwongoze hadi kwenye nyumba ya tajiri.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="14" prev="13" next="15" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="15" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Alisimama chini ya dirisha moja na kuanza kuimba wimbo wake alioupenda. Pole pole, kichwa cha tajiri kilionekana dirishani. {% include textcol_end.html %}

He stood below one big window and began to sing his favourite song. Slowly, the head of the rich man began to show through the big window.

Alisimama chini ya dirisha moja na kuanza kuimba wimbo wake alioupenda. Pole pole, kichwa cha tajiri kilionekana dirishani.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="15" prev="14" next="16" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="16" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Wafanyi kazi wote waliacha kazi zao wakamsikiliza Sakima. Hata hivyo, mwanamme mmoja alisema, “Hakuna aliyefaulu kumtuliza bwana. Je, huyu mvulana asiyeona ataweza kumtuliza?” {% include textcol_end.html %}

The workers stopped what they were doing. They listened to Sakima's beautiful song. But one man said, “Nobody has been able to console the boss. Does this blind boy think he will console him?”

Wafanyi kazi wote waliacha kazi zao wakamsikiliza Sakima. Hata hivyo, mwanamme mmoja alisema, “Hakuna aliyefaulu kumtuliza bwana. Je, huyu mvulana asiyeona ataweza kumtuliza?”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="16" prev="15" next="17" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="17" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Sakima alipomaliza kuimba wimbo wake alianza kuondoka. Tajiri alitoka nje kwa haraka na kusema, “Tafadhali, imba tena.” {% include textcol_end.html %}

Sakima finished singing his song and turned to leave. But the rich man rushed out and said, “Please sing again.”

Sakima alipomaliza kuimba wimbo wake alianza kuondoka. Tajiri alitoka nje kwa haraka na kusema, “Tafadhali, imba tena.”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="17" prev="16" next="18" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="18" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Wakati huo huo, watu wawili walikuja wakiwa wamembeba mtu kwenye machela. Walimkuta mwana wa tajiri akiwa amechapwa na kuachwa kando ya barabara. {% include textcol_end.html %}

At that very moment, two men came carrying someone on a stretcher. They had found the rich man's son beaten up and left on the side of the road.

Wakati huo huo, watu wawili walikuja wakiwa wamembeba mtu kwenye machela. Walimkuta mwana wa tajiri akiwa amechapwa na kuachwa kando ya barabara.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="18" prev="17" next="19" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="19" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Tajiri alifurahi sana kumwona mwana wake tena. Alimzawadi Sakima kwa kumliwaza. Aliwapeleka mwanawe na Sakima hosipitali ili Sakima aweze kusaidiwa kuona tena. {% include textcol_end.html %}

The rich man was so happy to see his son again. He rewarded Sakima for consoling him. He took his son and Sakima to hospital so Sakima could regain his sight.

Tajiri alifurahi sana kumwona mwana wake tena. Alimzawadi Sakima kwa kumliwaza. Aliwapeleka mwanawe na Sakima hosipitali ili Sakima aweze kusaidiwa kuona tena.
