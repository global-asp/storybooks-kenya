This is Khalai. She is seven years old. Her name means ‘the good one' in her language, Lubukusu.

Khalai ni msichana mwenye umri wa miaka saba. Jina lake lina maana ya ‘aliye mzuri’ katika lugha yake ya Lubukusu.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="02" prev="01" next="03" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="03" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Khalai wakes up and talks to the orange tree. “Please orange tree, grow big and give us lots of ripe oranges." {% include textcol_end.html %}

Khalai wakes up and talks to the orange tree. “Please orange tree, grow big and give us lots of ripe oranges."

Khalai anaamka na kuaongea na mmea mchanga wa Mchungwa, “Tafadhali mti wa Mchungwa, kua mkubwa uzae machungwa mengi mabivu.”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="03" prev="02" next="04" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="04" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Khalai walks to school. On the way she talks to the grass. “Please grass, grow greener and don't dry up." {% include textcol_end.html %}

Khalai walks to school. On the way she talks to the grass. “Please grass, grow greener and don't dry up."

Khalai anaenda shuleni na njiani anaongea na Nyasi, “Tafadhali Nyasi, kua kijani zaidi na zaidi na wala usikauke.”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="04" prev="03" next="05" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="05" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Khalai passes wild flowers. “Please flowers, keep blooming so I can put you in my hair." {% include textcol_end.html %}

Khalai passes wild flowers. “Please flowers, keep blooming so I can put you in my hair."

Khalai anayapita Maua ya mwituni na kusema, “Tafadhali Maua, endelea kunawiri ili niweze kukuweka kwenye nywele zangu.”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="05" prev="04" next="06" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="06" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} At school, Khalai talks to the tree in the middle of the compound. “Please tree, put out big branches so we can read under your shade." {% include textcol_end.html %}

At school, Khalai talks to the tree in the middle of the compound. “Please tree, put out big branches so we can read under your shade."

Akiwa shuleni, Khalai anauzungumzia Mti ulioko katikati ya shule, “Tafadhali Mti, zaa matawi makubwa ili tusomee chini ya kivuli chako.”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="06" prev="05" next="07" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="07" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Khalai talks to the hedge around her school. “Please grow strong and stop bad people from coming in." {% include textcol_end.html %}

Khalai talks to the hedge around her school. “Please grow strong and stop bad people from coming in."

Khalai anauzungumzia ua unaoizingira shule yake, “Tafadhali Ua, kua mwenye nguvu ili uzuie shule yetu kutokana na watu wabaya.”

{% include audiocontrols.html index="07" prev="06" next="08" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="08" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} When Khalai returns home from school, she visits the orange tree. “Are your oranges ripe yet?” asks Khalai. {% include textcol_end.html %}

When Khalai returns home from school, she visits the orange tree. “Are your oranges ripe yet?” asks Khalai.

Anaporudi nyumbani alasiri, Khalai anautembelea mti wa Mchungwa kuangalia ikiwa machungwa yameiva.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="08" prev="07" next="09" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="09" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} “The oranges are still green,” sighs Khalai. “I will see you tomorrow orange tree,” says Khalai. “Perhaps then you will have a ripe orange for me!” {% include textcol_end.html %}

“The oranges are still green,” sighs Khalai. “I will see you tomorrow orange tree,” says Khalai. “Perhaps then you will have a ripe orange for me!”

“Machungwa bado mabichi,” Khalai anashusha pumzi. “Nitakuona kesho, we mti Mchungwa. Labda utanipa chungwa mbivu wakati huo.”
