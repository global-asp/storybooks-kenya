Odongo and Apiyo lived in the city with their father. They looked forward to the holidays. Not just because school was closed, but because they went to visit their grandmother. She lived in a fishing village near a large lake.

Odongo na Apiyo waliishi jijini na baba yao. Wao walitazamia likizo. Siyo tu kuwa shule kufungwa, bali kwa sababu wangeenda kumtembelea nyanya yao. Aliishi katika kijiji cha uvuvi karibu na ziwa kubwa.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="02" prev="01" next="03" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="03" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Odongo fi Apiyon bayee gammadan sabaaban isaas akkoo isaani wanargatanif. Guyyaa tokko dura imala gara ganda ishee godhamuf qopha'an. Hirribnis oto isan hinfudhatin halkan guutuu wa'ee ayyannichaa hasa'a bulan. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Odongo and Apiyo were excited because it was time to visit their grandmother again. The night before, they packed their bags and got ready for the long journey to her village. They could not sleep and talked the whole night about the holiday.

Odongo na Apiyo walisisimka kwani ulikuwa wakati wa kumtembelea nyanya kwa mara nyingine. Usiku huo, walipanga mifuko yao tayari kwa safari ndefu ya kwenda kijijini. Hawakuweza kulala ila waliongea usiku kucha juu ya likizo yao.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="03" prev="02" next="04" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="04" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Barii sana konkolaata abbaa isaanitin deeman. Karaa isaani gubbatti benensoota daggalaa, garreen fi masii shayi bira kutani dhaqan. Imalaa isaani gubbtais sirbaa deeman. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Early the next morning, they left for the village in their father's car. They drove past mountains, wild animals and tea plantations. They counted cars and sang songs.

Mapema asubuhi iliyofuata, waliondoka katika gari la baba yao kuelekea kijijini. Walisafiri kupita milima, wanyama wa porini na mashamba ya majani chai. Walihesabu magari na kuimba nyimbo.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="04" prev="03" next="05" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="05" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Yeroo muraasa booda hirribini isaan fudhatee rafan. {% include textcol_end.html %}

After a while, the children were tired and fell asleep.

Baada ya muda, watoto walichoka na kulala.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="05" prev="04" next="06" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="06" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Yeroo gandaa gahan abban isaani hirribarra isan kaase. Yeroo ganda sana gahan Akkoon isaani Nyar-Kanyadan muka jala afaa afatee ciiftee jirti. Nyar-Kanyada jechuun itaala ummata Kaynada jechuu dha. Dubarti barredu fi cimtuu turtee. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Father woke up Odongo and Apiyo as they arrived in the village. They found Nyar-Kanyada, their grandmother, resting on a mat under a tree. Nyar-Kanyada in Luo, means ‘daughter of the people of Kanyada'. She was a strong and beautiful woman.

Baba aliwaamsha Odongo na Apiyo walipofika kijijini. Walimkuta nyanya yao, NyarKanyada, akipumzika kwenye mkeka chini ya mti. Katika Dholuo, Nyar-Kanyada inamaanisha ‘binti wa watu wa Kanyada’. Alikuwa mwanamke mwenye nguvu na mrembo.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="06" prev="05" next="07" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="07" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Nyar-Kanyadan sibaafi gammachuudhan isaan fudhaate. Akkakileen ishees kennaa magaalaa irra fidan kan akkoo isaanitif laatudhaf baye gammadan. " Dura kennaa ko banaa jette Odongon"." Lakki kiyya duraa banaa jedhee " Apiyoon. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Nyar-Kanyada welcomed them into the house and danced around the room singing with joy. Her grandchildren were excited to give her the presents they brought from the city. “First open my gift,” said Odongo. “No, my gift first!” said Apiyo.

Nyar-Kanyada alikwakaribisha nyumbani kwa kucheza na kuimba kwa furaha. Wajukuu wake walifurahi kumpatia zawadi walizoleta kutoka jijini. “Fungua yangu kwanza,” Odongo alisema. “La, zawadi yangu kwanza!” Apiyo akasema.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="07" prev="06" next="08" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="08" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Eegaa kenna sana bantee booda, Nyar-Kanyadan akaakilee ishee hala ummaa tureen ibifitee. {% include textcol_end.html %}

After she opened the presents, Nyar-Kanyada blessed her grandchildren in a traditional way.

Baada ya kufungua zawadi, Nyar-Kanyada aliwabariki wajukuu wake kitamaduni.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="08" prev="07" next="09" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="09" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Sana booda Odongo fi Apiyon gara alaa bahani billabillo fi simbrroo ari'aa taphatan. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Then Odongo and Apiyo went outside. They chased butterflies and birds.

Halafu Odongo na Apiyo walikwenda nje. Waliwakimbiza vipepeo na ndege.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="09" prev="08" next="10" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="10" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Muka gubbaa korani bishaan kessati kufan. {% include textcol_end.html %}

They climbed trees and splashed in the water of the lake.

Walipanda miti na kujirushia maji ziwani.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="10" prev="09" next="11" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="11" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Gara galagalaa gara manaatu irrbataaf debi'an. Oto nyataa hinxummurin hiribinni isan qabe. {% include textcol_end.html %}

When it was dark they returned to the house for dinner. Before they could finish eating, they were falling asleep!

Ilipokuwa giza, walirejea nyumbani kwa chakula cha jioni. Kabla kumaliza kula, walikuwa tayari wamelala!

{% include audiocontrols.html index="11" prev="10" next="12" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="12" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Barii isaa abban isaan ijoole sana Akko isaani birati disee gara magaalaa deebi'e. {% include textcol_end.html %}

The next day, the children's father drove back to the city leaving them with Nyar-Kanyada.

Siku iliyofuata, baba watoto aliondoka kurudi jijini huku akiwaacha na Nyar-Kanyada.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="12" prev="11" next="13" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="13" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Odongo fi Apiyon akkoo isaani hojii manaatin gargaaran.Buphaa fi kuddara sassabudhan gargaaran. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Odongo and Apiyo helped their grandmother with household chores. They fetched water and firewood. They collected eggs from the chickens and picked greens from the garden.

Odongo na Apiyo walimsaidia nyanya yao na kazi za nyumbani. Walitafuta kuni na kuchota maji. Walikusanya mayai na kuchuna mboga za majani kutoka kwenye bustani.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="13" prev="12" next="14" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="14" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Nyar-Kanyadan ijoole akakille ishee ugaali fi itto qopheesu barsistee. Ruzza akkati tolchani qurxxummi wajjiin nyatan ittiagrasiste. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Nyar-Kanyada taught her grandchildren to make soft ugali to eat with stew. She showed them how to make coconut rice to eat with roast fish.

Nyar-Kanyada aliwafunza wajukuu wake jinsi ya kupika ugali mwororo wa kula kwa kitoweo cha nyama. Aliwaonyesha namna ya kupika wali wa nazi wa kula kwa samaki wa kukaanga.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="14" prev="13" next="15" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="15" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Guyyaa ganama tokko Odongon sa'atti akko isaa gara margati ofee. sawaan suni gara masii ollati sente. Abbaan maasii sana bayee Odongoti aare.Bayee isaan lolee. Guyyaa sanairra calqabee gurbichi sanii akka masii namaa hinseene ofegganno gari godhe. {% include textcol_end.html %}

One morning, Odongo took his grandmother's cows to graze. They ran onto a neighbour's farm. The farmer was angry with Odongo. He threatened to keep the cows for eating his crops. After that day, the boy made sure that the cows did not get into trouble again.

Asubuhi moja, Odongo aliwapeleka ng'ombe wa nyanyake malishoni. Waliingia ndani ya shamba la jirani. Jirani alikasirishwa sana na Odongo. Alitishia kuwaweka ng'ombe kwa kula mimea yake. Baada ya siku hiyo, Odongo alihakikisha kwamba ng'ombe hawakosi tena.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="15" prev="14" next="16" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="16" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Yeroo biraa immo ijoolen Nyar-Kanyadan wajjiin gara gabbayaa deemtee. Isheenis kudraa fi samuna gurgurta qabde deemtee. Apiyon gatii meshaalee himmu calqabe. Ondongonim meeshaale maamiltonni bitan waltiqarqabaa ture. {% include textcol_end.html %}

On another day, the children went to the marketplace with Nyar-Kanyada. She had a stall selling vegetables, sugar and soap. Apiyo liked to tell customers the price of items. Odongo would pack the items that customers bought.

Siku nyingine watoto walikwenda sokoni pamoja na Nyar-Kanyada. Nyar-Kanyada alikuwa na kibanda cha kuuza mboga, sukari na sabuni. Apiyo alipenda kuwaelezea wateja bei za bidhaa. Odongo naye aliwafungia wateja bidhaa walizonunua.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="16" prev="15" next="17" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="17" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Gara galgalaa shahii chayi wajjiin dhugan. Akkoo isaani maallaqa lakka'udhan gargaran. {% include textcol_end.html %}

At the end of the day they drank chai tea together. They helped grandmother to count the money she earned.

Mwishoni mwa siku, walikunywa chai pamoja. Walimsaidia nyanya yao kuhesabu pesa alizopata siku hiyo.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="17" prev="16" next="18" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="18" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Osoo bayee hinturin yeroon boqqonna isaani dhumee ijoolen gara magaalaa debite.Nyar-Kanyadan odongo dhaf kophii Apiyoodhaf immo shurrabi kenetef. Dhiyana oolchaa isaanif laatte. {% include textcol_end.html %}

But too soon the holidays were over and the children had to go back to the city. Nyar-Kanyada gave Odongo a cap and Apiyo a sweater. She packed food for their journey.

Lakini likizo ilikaribia kuisha na watoto walilazimika kurudi jijini. Nyar-Kanyada alimpatia Odongo kofia na Apiyo sweta. Aliwafungia chakula cha safarini.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="18" prev="17" next="19" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="19" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Yeroo abban isaani isaan fudhachudhaf dhufu ijoolen deemu hinbarbanne. Ijoolen Nyar-Kanyadan akka isaani waliin demtu kadhaate. Ishe akka kofuu jette, akkan jetten, " Ani magalaa deemuudhaf bayeen dulomme kanaafu ani asumatin isin eega. {% include textcol_end.html %}

When their father came to fetch them, they did not want to leave. The children begged Nyar-Kanyada to go with them to the city. She smiled and said, “I am too old for the city. I will be waiting for you to come to my village again.”

Baba yao alipofika kuwachukuwa, hawakutak kuondoka. Walimshihi Nyar-Kanyada kwenda nao jijini. Alitabasamu na kusema, "Nimezeeka sana siwezi kwenda jijini. Nitawasubiri mje hapa kijijini tena."

{% include audiocontrols.html index="19" prev="18" next="20" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="20" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Odongo fi Apiyon hammatani naggati ta'ii jedhanin. {% include textcol_end.html %}

Odongo and Apiyo both hugged her tightly and said goodbye.

Odongo na Apiyo walimbusu na kumuaga.

{% include audiocontrols.html index="20" prev="19" next="21" %}
{% include picturecol.html index="21" %} {% include textcol_start.html %} Yeroo Odongo fi Apiyon gara mana barumsaa dhaqan wa'ee badiyaa hiriyota isaanit himan. Ijoolen tokko jireenya magaala dansaa jedhani yadaan. kun immo badiyyan dansaa dha jechuu calqaban. Hundi isaani garu Odongo for Apiyon akko dansaa akka qabaan waligalan. {% include textcol_end.html %}

When Odongo and Apiyo went back to school they told their friends about life in the village. Some children felt that life in the city was good. Others felt that the village was better. But most of all, everyone agreed that Odongo and Apiyo had a wonderful grandmother!

Odongo na Apiyo walirudi shuleni, waliwaambia marafiki zao kuhusu maisha kijijini. Baadhi ya watoto waliamini kuwa maisha ya jijini ni bora. Wengine waliamini maisha kijijini ni bora zaidi. Lakini zaidi ya yote, kila mmoja alikubali kwamba Odongo na Apiyo walikuwa na nyanya wa ajabu.
